My favorite books

Metamorphoses

Ovid

A Roman poetic epic turning Greek myth into a swirling study of transformation and desire.

Simulacra and Simulation

Jean Baudrillard

Arguing how media makes appearance replace reality, and truth becomes slippery.

Thus Spoke Zarathustra

Friedrich Nietsche

A wandering sage urges humanity to transform itself and surpass old values.

Beyond Good and Evil

Friedrich Nietzsche

A provocative tour through morality’s illusions and the courage to think dangerously.

Omnicide

Jason Bahbak Mohaghegh

A feverish philosophical descent into annihilation through war diaries, mysticism, and surreal violence.

Difference and Repetition

Gilles Deleuze

Explaining how real novelty emerges, arguing that philosophy should focus on creative difference rather than identity or representation.

Process and Reality

Alfred North Whitehead

Proposong a process-based metaphysics where reality is composed of interrelated events and experiences instead of stable substances.

The Aeneid

Virgil

Virgil’s imperial founding myth, foreshadowing Rome’s glory and blood.

Madness and Civilization

Michel Foucault

How institutions “invent” madness by policing difference across European history.

Discipline and Punish

Michel Foucault

Tracing the historical shift from public torture to modern institutions that regulate behavior through surveillance and routine.

After Finitude: An Essay on the Necessity of Contingency

Quentin Meillassoux

Part of the OOO lineage, arguing that philosophy escape human-centered 'correlationism,' where contingency underlies everything.

On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects

Gilbert Simondon

Machines evolving and individuating alongside humans.

Object Oriented Ontology: a New Theory of Everything

Graham Harman

Seminal OOO work, saying that objects always withdraw and relations never capture reality’s hidden depth.

Libidinal Economy

Jean-François Lyotard

Fusings markets, bodies, and history pulse with libidinal drives.

Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid

Douglas Hofstadter

Linking logic, music, and art to explore self-reference and consciousness emergence.

My favorite movies & shows

Stalker (1979)

Andrei Tarkovsky

Three men travel into a restricted Zone rumored to fulfill desires, but the film uses the spiritual journey to examine belief, despair, and the difficulty of confronting one's inner life.

Monster (2004)

Naoki Urasawa

A brilliant doctor saves a boy who grows into a methodical killer, forcing him into a long pursuit across post-Cold-War Europe that exposes institutional cruelty, psychological manipulation, and the frightening fragility of moral certainty.

Serial Experiments Lain (1998)

Yasuyuki Ueda

A teenager is drawn into a network world where digital communication dissolves identity and the boundary between personal consciousness and collective reality breaks down.

Mindwalk (1990)

Bernt Capra

A poet, politician, and physicist debate systems theory and responsibility in Mont-Saint-Michel’s ruins.
